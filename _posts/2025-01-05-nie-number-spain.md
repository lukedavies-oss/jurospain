## NIE vs NIF vs TIE: decoding the acronyms

Spain loves acronyms. Understanding them keeps your paperwork aligned:

* **NIE (Número de Identidad de Extranjero).** A unique tax identification number assigned to non-Spaniards. It never expires and appears on most immigration documents.
* **NIF (Número de Identificación Fiscal).** The fiscal number for Spanish citizens. When a foreigner has an NIE, it doubles as their NIF.
* **TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero).** A physical biometric residence card for non-EU residents. Your NIE appears on the TIE, but you can have an NIE without a TIE.

Think of the NIE as your core identifier. Even if you pursue residency later, the NIE is the first building block.

## Who needs an NIE in 2025?

You must obtain an NIE if you plan to engage in almost any financial or legal transaction in Spain. That includes:

* Purchasing property or a vehicle
* Opening a bank account or signing a mortgage
* Starting a business or registering as autónomo
* Working or paying tax in Spain
* Inheriting assets or receiving official payments

EU citizens can usually get the NIE together with their EU registration certificate, but many prefer to secure it beforehand for banking or property steps. Non-EU citizens often receive an NIE automatically when their residency is approved, but property buyers, power of attorney holders, or investors may need it earlier. Having the NIE ready speeds up subsequent applications like the Golden Visa or Digital Nomad Visa.

## Where to apply: Spain or abroad?

You have two main pathways:

1. **In Spain at a National Police station (Comisaría de Policía) or foreigners’ office.** Ideal if you’re already in Spain or have a representative with power of attorney. Appointments are booked on the *cita previa* system.
2. **At a Spanish consulate abroad.** Useful when you need the NIE before arriving—common for property purchases. Consulates follow Spain’s rules but may have extra requirements, so check their website before applying.

If you choose Spain, target the province where you will carry out the economic activity. Madrid and Barcelona have high demand but also more appointment slots. Smaller provinces may offer faster availability if you can justify the link (for example, buying property there).

## Documents checklist (EU and non-EU)

Prepare clean, legible copies of everything plus originals. Typical requirements include:

* **Passport** (valid for at least six months) and photocopy of the ID page.
* **Completed EX-15 form** printed and signed in blue ink.
* **Proof of economic or social interest**—for example, a property reservation contract, employment offer, company incorporation documents, or a sworn statement explaining the need.
* **Two passport-sized photos** (some offices request them).
* **Tasa payment receipt (Modelo 790 Código 012).** Bring the bank-stamped copy.
* **Appointment confirmation printout.**

Non-EU applicants applying from Spain may also need proof of legal entry (entry stamp or boarding pass). If someone is applying on your behalf, add a notarised power of attorney translated into Spanish.

Consulates often require applications to be submitted in person, though some accept courier submissions. Expect them to request certified translations of supporting documents and possibly proof of address in their jurisdiction.

## How to complete form EX-15

EX-15 asks for basic identification and the reason for requesting an NIE. Key tips:

1. **Section 1 – Applicant details.** Write your names exactly as they appear on your passport. Use capital letters and include marital status.
2. **Section 2 – Representative.** Fill this only if someone else will appear for you. Attach their ID and power of attorney.
3. **Section 3 – Address in Spain.** If you don’t yet have one, provide a temporary address such as your future property, lawyer’s office, or hotel (confirm they are comfortable receiving mail).
4. **Section 4 – Reason.** Select the relevant box (economic, professional, or social interests) and describe your objective clearly: “Compra de vivienda en Madrid”, “Apertura de cuenta bancaria”, or “Solicitud de visado nómada digital”.
5. **Signature and date.** Sign within the box, using the European format (day/month/year).

Bring two copies of the form. Officers typically stamp one and return it as confirmation.

## Paying the tasa: Modelo 790 Código 012

The administrative fee (tasa) for 2025 is €9.84 in most regions. To pay it:

1. Generate the form at [policia.es](https://sede.policia.gob.es) > *Procedimientos > Extranjería* > *Asignación de NIE*.
2. Fill in personal details and select “Asignación de Número de Identidad de Extranjero (NIE) a instancia del interesado”.
3. Print the three-page form and take it to any bank. Pay in cash and ask for the stamped copy (*Ejemplar para la Administración*).
4. Some banks allow online payment if you already have a Spanish account. Save the PDF receipt.

Without the stamped proof, the police will not process your application. Double-check the amount in case regional updates occur.

## Booking your appointment

Appointments are booked through the [cita previa platform](https://sede.administracionespublicas.gob.es/icpplus/). Steps:

1. Select your province and the procedure “Asignación de NIE”.
2. Enter passport details and the verification code.
3. Choose a time slot. New slots often appear early mornings (08:00–09:00) Monday through Thursday.
4. Print or screenshot the confirmation email.

If you cannot find availability, expand your search to nearby provinces, use different browsers, or work with a legal representative who monitors releases. Some provinces release cancellations late at night. Be cautious with third-party services that sell slots; many violate terms and risk cancellation.

## Timelines and what happens on the day

* **Arrival.** Arrive 15 minutes early with all originals and copies. Dress neatly—officers appreciate professionalism.
* **Submission.** Hand over the EX-15, passport, tasa receipt, and supporting documents. Officers may ask a few questions about your reason for applying.
* **Outcome.** In many cities you receive the NIE on the spot, printed on a sheet with a stamp. Other offices ask you to return in a few days or provide it by email.

Typical timelines:

* **Madrid & Barcelona:** Appointment wait 2–4 weeks. Same-day issuance or 5 business days.
* **Valencia & Alicante:** 1–2 week wait; same-day issuance.
* **Consulates:** 10–20 business days after submission.

Keep the NIE certificate safe and scan a digital copy. The number never changes, so you can reuse it for future procedures.

## Troubleshooting common issues

**No appointments available.** Ask our team to monitor releases or consider applying in a neighbouring province linked to your property or investment.

**Documents rejected.** Officers can request additional evidence. Provide a notarised purchase contract, bank pre-approval, or employer letter showing the need.

**Expired passport.** Renew before applying; some offices accept passports with less than six months validity only if you show renewal proof.

**Consulate delays.** Follow up weekly via email. Provide proof of urgency (e.g., property completion date) to prioritise your case.

**Lost NIE certificate.** Request a duplicate at the same office with your passport and proof of the original issuance. The number stays the same.

## FAQs

### How long does the NIE last?

Indefinitely. The number is yours for life, even if you change addresses or residency status.

### Can someone apply on my behalf?

Yes. Provide a notarised power of attorney (in Spanish) authorising a representative to submit the EX-15 and collect the certificate.

### Do I need an NIE to open a bank account?

Most banks request it, although some will open an account with your passport and a promise to provide the NIE within 30–60 days. Having the NIE upfront speeds things up.

### What if I already have a TIE?

Your TIE already contains your NIE. Keep using that number. If you misplace the card, request a duplicate TIE rather than a new NIE.

### Can I convert a temporary NIE into residency?

An NIE is only an identifier. To become a resident you must apply for the appropriate visa or registration. However, having the NIE is a prerequisite for those steps.
