## Resident vs non-resident accounts

* **Non-resident accounts** are ideal while you’re scouting property or waiting on your visa. They allow deposits, payments, and mortgage processing but may have higher fees and limited services (e.g., no overdrafts).
* **Resident accounts** unlock better fees, digital banking features, and payroll services. You can convert once you hold a residency card or certificate of registration (EU citizens).

Banks must classify your residency status for anti-money laundering purposes. Updating your status promptly avoids frozen accounts.

## Documents every bank will ask for

Most banks request:

* Passport and photocopy.
* NIE certificate or application proof (some accept the NIE letter without the physical card).
* Proof of address (rental contract, utility bill, or hotel invoice for non-residents).
* Proof of income or employment (payslips, tax returns, company letter).
* Tax identification number from your home country.

Bring originals and copies. If documents are not in Spanish, provide translations—especially for income proof.

## Getting the non-resident certificate

Non-resident accounts require a **Certificado de No Residente** issued by the National Police. Steps:

1. Book an appointment at the police station (procedure: “Certificados y asignación NIE”).
2. Submit passport, completed EX-15 (or specific form), and pay the tasa (Modelo 790 Código 012, €7.43 in 2025).
3. Collect the certificate within 5–10 days. Some banks handle the process for a fee; ask during your appointment.

The certificate is valid for 90 days. Banks must re-verify your status every two years; expect a small fee for the renewal (usually €15–€20 debited automatically).

## Opening online vs in branch

* **Online:** Some banks (BBVA, Revolut Spain, N26) allow digital onboarding for residents. Upload documents, take selfies, and sign electronically. Non-residents often face roadblocks due to extra checks.
* **In branch:** Traditional route. Schedule an appointment, bring printed documents, and sign forms in person. Banks may open the account immediately and issue a temporary debit card on the spot.

## Fees, maintenance, and currency transfers

* Monthly maintenance fees range from €0–€15 depending on account type and whether you meet conditions (salary deposit, minimum balance).
* International transfers via SWIFT incur €15–€30; consider using SEPA transfers or fintech services (Wise, Revolut) for lower rates.
* ATM withdrawals are free at your bank’s network; other ATMs may charge €1.5–€2.5.
* Currency exchange: monitor rates and consider multi-currency accounts if you receive income in GBP or USD.

## Switching to a resident account

Once you obtain residency:

1. Visit the branch with your TIE or EU registration certificate.
2. Update your tax residency status (Modelo 720 obligations may apply if you hold assets abroad over €50,000).
3. Request resident account benefits—reduced fees, overdrafts, or investment services.
4. Update direct debits and employer payroll details.

Banks may require proof of social security affiliation or employment when switching.

## Banking tips for property buyers

* Open the account before signing the *arras* contract to transfer deposits quickly.
* Request banker’s drafts (*cheques bancarios*) for notary day—order them 48 hours in advance.
* Coordinate with the bank’s mortgage department early; they will need property details for valuation.
* Keep large incoming transfers documented (source of funds statements) to avoid compliance holds.

## Keeping your account compliant

* Respond promptly to bank KYC requests. They often send emails requesting updated income proofs or tax forms.
* File Spain’s **Modelo 720** if you become tax resident and hold foreign assets exceeding €50,000.
* Maintain sufficient balances to avoid dormant account fees.
* Notify the bank if you change address or phone number to keep online banking access secure.

## FAQs

### Can I open an account before I have an NIE?

Some banks allow it with a passport and a promise to provide the NIE within 90 days. However, most will freeze the account if the NIE is not supplied.

### Do online banks accept non-residents?

Generally no. Digital-first banks require Spanish residency documentation. Stick with traditional banks until you secure residency.

### How long does it take to open an account?

In-branch appointments typically take 45–60 minutes. Accounts can be active the same day once compliance approves your file.

### Will my foreign income be taxed in Spain?

Only if you become a tax resident. Non-residents pay Spanish tax on Spanish-source income only.

### Can I hold joint accounts?

Yes. Both parties must attend the appointment with their documents. Joint accounts are useful for couples buying property together.
