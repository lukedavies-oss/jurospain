## Why choose Spain’s Golden Visa?

* **Mobility.** Live and work in Spain with access to the Schengen Area for 90/180-day travel.
* **Family coverage.** Include spouses, unmarried partners, dependent children (including students), and dependent parents.
* **Flexible stay requirement.** Visit Spain once during each permit period—no strict minimum stay until you aim for permanent residency or citizenship.
* **Real estate upside.** Combine lifestyle and investment by purchasing in growth markets such as Valencia, Málaga, or the Balearic Islands.

## Who qualifies in 2025?

Key criteria:

* Non-EU, non-EEA, non-Swiss citizenship.
* Clean criminal record for the past five years in any country of residence.
* Private health insurance valid in Spain.
* Proof of sufficient economic means to support yourself and dependants.
* Qualifying investment completed before (or conditional upon) the application submission.

Investors must be at least 18 years old and not listed as undesirable in Spain. Applications can be filed inside Spain or through the consulate in your home country. If you are already in Spain on a tourist visa, you may switch to the investor permit during your stay (within legal terms of entry).

## Investment options explained

1. **Real estate investment.** Purchase property worth at least €500,000 free of liens. You may buy one or several properties. Mortgages are permitted beyond the first €500,000.
2. **Business project.** Present a plan considered of general interest by the Directorate-General for International Trade and Investments. Focus on job creation, socio-economic impact, or technological innovation.
3. **Capital investments.** Deposit €1 million in a Spanish bank, buy €1 million of Spanish company shares, or invest €2 million in Spanish government bonds.

Real estate remains the most popular option due to tangible value and immediate lifestyle benefits. Business projects require detailed proposals and can take longer to evaluate.

## Property purchase workflow

1. **Reservation and due diligence.** Sign a reservation contract (*reserva*) and order a *nota simple* to confirm ownership, charges, and urban planning compliance. We coordinate bilingual lawyers and surveyors.
2. **Deposit contract (*arras*).** Typically 10% of the purchase price. Include conditions that allow you to recover funds if the Golden Visa is refused.
3. **Financing.** If using a mortgage, obtain a bank pre-approval. Non-residents usually receive 60–70% loan-to-value with interest rates between 3.5–4.5% in 2025.
4. **Completion at the notary.** Sign the deed (*escritura*), settle taxes (ITP or VAT + AJD), and register the property.
5. **Proof of investment.** Collect the notarised deed, land registry entry, and bank payment proofs—these form the backbone of your Golden Visa file.

We ensure every document is translated, apostilled if needed, and digitised for the telematic submission.

## Due diligence and background checks

The consulate or UGE-CE (Unidad de Grandes Empresas) will verify:

* Criminal record certificates (apostilled and translated).
* Proof that investment funds originated legally.
* Up-to-date tax compliance in Spain if you already hold assets.
* Absence of prior immigration breaches.

Be prepared to provide bank statements, company records, or contracts demonstrating the legitimate origin of funds. Our team reviews these documents upfront to avoid last-minute surprises.

## Family reunification and dependants

You can apply jointly or add family members after the principal investor’s permit is granted. Include:

* Spouse or registered partner (provide marriage certificate or evidence of partnership).
* Children under 18 or over 18 who are economically dependent and studying.
* Dependent parents or in-laws over 65 (exceptionally under 65 with proof of dependency).

Each dependant requires their own health insurance, criminal record certificate (if over 16), and evidence of dependency. For blended families, provide custody agreements or notarised consent from the other parent.

## Application documents and translations

Core documents for the principal applicant:

* Passport (minimum validity one year).
* NIE number (we can obtain it before or alongside the application).
* Form **EX-01** for investors (if applying inside Spain) or national visa form (outside Spain).
* Proof of investment (deeds, bank certificates, share purchase contracts).
* Tax identification and bank payment evidence showing at least €500,000 unencumbered.
* Health insurance policy issued by a Spanish provider with no co-payments.
* Background checks from countries lived in during the past five years.
* Proof of financial means (bank statements, dividends, employment contracts).

All foreign documents must be legalised or apostilled and translated by a sworn translator into Spanish. Plan translation timelines early—peak relocation seasons create bottlenecks.

## Fees, taxes, and holding requirements

Budget for the following (approximate 2025 figures):

* **Property taxes.** Resales incur 6–10% Transfer Tax (ITP) depending on the region; new builds require 10% VAT plus 1–1.5% Stamp Duty (AJD).
* **Notary and land registry.** Around 0.5–1% combined, depending on property price.
* **Legal fees.** 1–1.5% of the purchase price for full-service conveyancing.
* **Golden Visa fees.** Government fees around €75 for the visa plus €80–€90 per residence card; expect higher for family bundles.
* **Maintenance.** Keep the investment for the life of your permit. Selling the property without reinvesting at least €500,000 in another asset ends eligibility.

You do not become a tax resident unless you spend more than 183 days per year in Spain or have your centre of economic interests here. Nevertheless, property ownership triggers local taxes (IBI), insurance, and non-resident income tax filings.

## Renewals, long-term residency, and citizenship

* **Initial residence authorisation:** 3 years if applying from Spain (visa + residence card). Consulate route grants a one-year visa followed by a two-year residence card.
* **Renewals:** Every five years after the initial term. You must show the investment is maintained, health insurance remains in force, and you have visited Spain at least once.
* **Permanent residency:** Eligible after five years of continuous legal residence (with proof of staying at least six months per year).
* **Citizenship:** Typically after ten years of legal residence, reduced for nationals of certain countries (e.g., two years for many Latin American citizens). Meeting citizenship requirements involves integrating into Spanish society and demonstrating language proficiency (DELE A2) and knowledge (CCSE exam).

## Timelines, tips, and common pitfalls

**Planning timeline:**

1. Week 1–2: Property search, reservation, NIE acquisition.
2. Week 3–6: Due diligence, mortgage arrangements, background checks.
3. Week 7–8: Notary completion, translation, apostille.
4. Week 9: File Golden Visa application telematically via UGE or at the consulate.
5. Weeks 10–14: Resolution (usually within 20 working days for UGE).

**Avoid these pitfalls:**

* **Incomplete translation pack.** Missing apostilles or sworn translations delay approvals. We build a master checklist and liaise with translators daily.
* **Tax surprises.** Buyers forget about ITP/AJD or ongoing community fees. We model full cash flows, including insurance and utilities.
* **Tight completion dates.** Leave at least 15 days between notary signing and visa submission to collect certified documents.
* **Unregistered partners.** Spain accepts stable partnerships with documentation; gather joint leases, bank accounts, or notarial certificates in advance.

**Value of professional support:** Our concierge coordinates sellers, banks, notaries, translators, and the immigration authority. You receive weekly status reports and a secure portal for sharing documents.

## FAQs

### Can I rent out the property?

Yes. You can rent long-term or short-term subject to local licensing rules. Income must be declared via Spanish tax returns (Modelo 210 for non-residents).

### Do I need to live in Spain permanently?

No. You only need to visit once per permit period to keep the Golden Visa active. If you aim for permanent residency or citizenship, plan to spend more than 183 days per year.

### What happens if property values change?

As long as you invested at least €500,000 initially and continue to own the property, market fluctuations do not affect your permit.

### Can I finance part of the investment?

Yes. Mortgages are allowed above the first €500,000. The debt-free portion must remain unencumbered.

### How fast is the approval?

UGE typically responds within 20 working days. Consulate applications may take 4–8 weeks depending on workload.
