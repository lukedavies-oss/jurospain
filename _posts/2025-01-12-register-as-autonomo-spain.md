## Who should register as autónomo

Register if you plan to:

* Invoice Spanish clients regularly.
* Operate a freelance or consulting business from Spain.
* Run an e-commerce shop or rental business.
* Provide professional services (law, design, coaching) as an individual.

Occasional activity may not require registration, but Spanish authorities consider frequency and intent. When in doubt, register to avoid fines.

## Pre-registration checklist

1. **NIE and empadronamiento.** Ensure your identification and address are up to date.
2. **Spanish bank account.** Needed for social security payments and tax refunds.
3. **Digital certificate or [Cl@ve PIN](https://clave.gob.es).** Enables online filings with Hacienda and Social Security.
4. **Business plan basics.** Define services, expected turnover, and expense categories.
5. **Insurance review.** Professional liability cover and health insurance (if not covered elsewhere).

## Step-by-step: registering with Hacienda

1. **Submit Form 036 or 037** (simplified version) online or in person at the Agencia Tributaria office.
2. **Choose your tax epigraph (IAE).** This code describes your economic activity.
3. **Indicate VAT obligations.** Most services charge 21% IVA; some professional or educational services are exempt.
4. **Select accounting method.** Standard is direct estimation (*estimación directa simplificada*) for freelancers.
5. **Register start date.** You can backdate up to 60 days if needed, but we recommend aligning with your first invoice.

Hacienda will issue your tax registration certificate instantly if done online.

## Step-by-step: joining Social Security (RETA)

1. **Within 60 days** of Hacienda registration, submit the RETA application via the Social Security portal (or within 30 days of starting activity).
2. Provide bank account details for monthly quota payments.
3. Choose your contribution base (between the minimum and maximum). New autónomos can select the minimum base initially.
4. Confirm coverage options (professional contingencies, cessation of activity, training).
5. Receive confirmation and access your payment schedule.

From 2023 onwards, Spain introduced income-based quotas. You must estimate annual net income when registering and update it if your income changes.

## Understanding social security quotas

2025 monthly quotas range from approximately €230 to €500 depending on declared income. New autónomos benefit from the **tarifa plana**: €80/month for the first 12 months, extendable if your net income remains below the minimum wage. After that, quotas adjust according to income bands.

Late payments incur surcharges of 10–20%. Set up automatic debits and calendar reminders.

## VAT (IVA) and income tax obligations

* **Quarterly VAT (Modelo 303).** Declare VAT collected on invoices minus VAT paid on expenses. Pay the difference by the 20th of April, July, October, and January.
* **Annual VAT summary (Modelo 390).** Recap of the year’s VAT operations, submitted in January.
* **Quarterly income tax payments (Modelo 130).** Pay 20% of net profit every quarter unless 70% of your invoices have withholding tax.
* **Annual income tax return (Modelo 100).** Due between April and June each year.
* **Withholding obligations.** If you pay other freelancers, withhold 15% IRPF and submit Modelo 111 (quarterly) and Modelo 190 (annual).

## Bookkeeping and digital invoicing

Spain is moving towards mandatory e-invoicing for B2B transactions. Best practices:

* Use invoicing software compliant with anti-fraud laws (Veri*Factu-ready).
* Keep digital copies of invoices and receipts for five years.
* Track deductible expenses: coworking, software, travel (within limits), utilities (partial deduction for home offices).
* Reconcile bank statements monthly to avoid surprises at tax time.

## When to hire a gestor or advisor

Consider professional support if:

* You invoice in multiple countries and need double-taxation planning.
* You lack time to file quarterly returns.
* You plan to hire employees or subcontractors.
* You want guidance on deductions, grants, or EU funding.

Gestores typically charge €60–€120 per month for routine filings. Juro Spain partners with bilingual advisors familiar with international clients.

## Scaling to a company (SL) later

If turnover exceeds €80,000–€100,000 or you need liability protection, consider incorporating an **Sociedad Limitada (SL)**. Steps include:

1. Reserving a company name (Registro Mercantil Central).
2. Opening a business bank account and depositing share capital (€1 or more under the new law, though €3,000 is still common).
3. Drafting bylaws and signing the incorporation deed before a notary.
4. Registering with Hacienda and Social Security for corporate obligations.

You can remain as autónomo administrator but will have different tax filings (IS, VAT, payroll).

## FAQs

### Do I need to register if my clients are abroad?

Yes, if you are delivering work from Spain on a regular basis. You may apply reverse-charge VAT rules for EU clients, but Hacienda still expects registration.

### Can I deduct home office expenses?

Yes, up to 30% of proportional utility costs when you declare part of your home as office space. Keep utility bills and note the percentage used for work.

### What happens if I stop working temporarily?

File a cessation of activity (*baja*) with Hacienda and Social Security to pause obligations. You can reactivate later with Form 036/037 and RETA re-registration.

### Is private health insurance mandatory?

Once you pay autónomo quotas, you gain access to public healthcare. Many expats keep private insurance for faster appointments.

### Can I hire employees as autónomo?

Yes. Register as an employer (*empresario individual*), file additional Social Security forms, and manage payroll taxes. A gestor can handle the setup.
