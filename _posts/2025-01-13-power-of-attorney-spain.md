If you can’t attend every signing in Spain, a power of attorney (PoA or *poder notarial*) keeps your plans on track. From property purchases to NIE applications, a well-drafted PoA empowers a trusted representative to act on your behalf. This 2025 guide explains when powers of attorney are needed, how to grant them from abroad, and how to stay protected.

## When you need a Spanish power of attorney

PoAs are common for:

* Property purchases and sales when you can’t attend the notary.
* Golden Visa and non-lucrative visa filings via representatives.
* Mortgage signings, bank account openings, and NIE applications.
* Court proceedings or administrative appeals.

Granting a PoA avoids rescheduling critical appointments and keeps transactions moving while you remain abroad.

## Choosing your attorney-in-fact

Select someone you trust implicitly—often your lawyer, a Juro Spain representative, or a family member. Confirm they have valid identification (passport, DNI, or NIE) and are comfortable attending appointments in Spanish.

You may appoint more than one attorney, allowing them to act jointly or separately. Joint powers require both to sign every document; separate powers provide flexibility but require strong trust.

## Drafting essentials and clauses

A robust PoA should include:

* **Your details.** Full name, nationality, passport/NIE number, marital status, and address.
* **Attorney’s details.** Same level of identification for your representative.
* **Scope of powers.** Specify actions allowed (e.g., buying property at a specific address, applying for NIE, signing mortgage, representing before tax authorities).
* **Duration.** Indicate whether the PoA is general, limited to a transaction, or revocable at any time.
* **Substitution clause.** Decide whether your attorney can delegate powers to another person.
* **Conflict-of-interest safeguards.** Limit authority to necessary actions and require reporting back after each step.

Our team drafts bilingual PoAs tailored to your transaction so notaries in Spain and abroad understand the scope clearly.

## Granting the PoA in Spain vs abroad

**In Spain:**

* Visit a Spanish notary with your passport/NIE.
* Sign the PoA drafted by your lawyer or us.
* Receive authorised copies (*copias autorizadas*) stamped with the notary’s seal.

**Abroad:**

* Visit a local notary public. Provide the Spanish/English draft.
* Sign the document; the notary will witness and seal it.
* Legalise with an apostille (under the Hague Convention) or Spanish consulate legalisation if your country is not a signatory.

Schedule notary appointments early—many require in-person bookings and identification verification.

## Notarisation, apostille, and translation

* **Apostille:** Attaches to the original PoA confirming the notary’s authority. Processing times vary (same-day in some countries, weeks in others).
* **Sworn translation:** If the PoA isn’t in Spanish, a sworn translator must translate it before Spanish authorities accept it.
* **Multiple copies:** Request several originals if multiple institutions need the PoA; each notary office will keep one.

## Delivering and using the PoA

Send the apostilled original (and translation) to Spain via courier. Your attorney will present it at:

* The notary on completion day.
* Police stations for NIE appointments.
* Banks for mortgage or account opening.
* Government offices for immigration procedures.

Most institutions keep the original; ensure you retain scanned copies for your records.

## Risks, safeguards, and revocation

* **Trust and oversight.** Choose reputable attorneys and request status updates. We provide written summaries after each action.
* **Limited scope.** Avoid overly broad powers—tie authority to specific transactions or actions.
* **Revocation.** You can revoke the PoA at any time by signing a revocation deed before a notary and notifying the attorney and relevant institutions.
* **Expiry.** Some PoAs include automatic expiry upon completion of a transaction or after a set date.

If you suspect misuse, notify the notary who authorised the PoA and the relevant authorities immediately.

## FAQs

### Can I grant a power of attorney digitally?

Spain still requires wet ink signatures before a notary. Some countries allow remote online notarisation, but Spain will only accept the PoA once it carries a physical apostille and sworn translation if needed.

### How long does the process take?

Drafting takes 2–3 days. Apostilles abroad can take 1–3 weeks, so plan accordingly. In Spain, the PoA is ready the same day you sign at the notary.

### Can I limit the PoA to one property?

Yes. Specify the property address, purchase price range, and related tasks to keep the authority narrow.

### Do both spouses need to sign?

If assets are jointly owned or you’re married under community property regimes, both spouses should sign the PoA or grant mutual authorisations.

### How do I revoke the PoA?

Sign a revocation deed before a notary (in Spain or abroad), apostille if necessary, and notify the attorney and institutions. Request the original PoA be returned or marked as revoked.
