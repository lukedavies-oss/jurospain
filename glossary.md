# Spanish Legal & Immigration Glossary

Your quick-reference guide to Spanish legal and immigration terminology. All terms explained in plain English.

---

## A

**Abogado**  
Lawyer or attorney. Required for most property purchases and complex immigration matters in Spain.

**AJD (Actos Jurídicos Documentados)**  
Stamp duty tax applied to certain legal documents, including new property purchases and mortgages. Typically 1-1.5% of property value.

**Apostille**  
International certificate authenticating documents for use abroad. Required for most foreign documents submitted to Spanish authorities under the Hague Convention.

**Autónomo**  
Self-employed or freelance worker registered with Spanish Social Security. Required if you work for yourself in Spain.

**Ayuntamiento**  
Town hall or city council. Where you register your address (empadronamiento) and handle local administrative matters.

---

## B

**Beckham Law (Ley Beckham)**  
Special tax regime allowing new Spanish residents to pay flat 24% tax on Spanish income instead of progressive rates for up to six years. Named after footballer David Beckham.

**BOE (Boletín Oficial del Estado)**  
Spain's official state gazette where laws, regulations, and official announcements are published.

---

## C

**Catastro**  
Land registry office that maintains property records including cadastral values used for tax calculations.

**Cédula de Habitabilidad**  
Certificate of habitability confirming a property meets minimum standards for human occupation. Required in some regions for property sales and rentals.

**Certificado de Empadronamiento**  
Certificate proving your registered address at the town hall. Essential for visa renewals, healthcare, school enrollment, and many official procedures.

**Certificado Digital**  
Digital certificate allowing you to conduct official business online with Spanish government agencies. Like a digital signature.

**CCSE Exam**  
Constitutional and sociocultural knowledge test required for permanent residency and citizenship applications. Covers Spanish history, culture, and government.

**Comunidad de Propietarios**  
Homeowners' association for apartment buildings and complexes. Manages common areas and collects monthly fees (gastos de comunidad).

**Consejería**  
Regional government department responsible for specific policy areas. Immigration offices often fall under regional consejería jurisdiction.

---

## D

**DELE (Diplomas de Español como Lengua Extranjera)**  
Official Spanish language proficiency certificates. A2 level required for permanent residency; B1 for citizenship.

**Digital Nomad Visa**  
Residence permit allowing remote workers to live in Spain while working for non-Spanish employers or clients. Maximum 20% of income can come from Spanish sources.

---

## E

**Empadronamiento**  
Official registration of your residential address at the local town hall. Legally required for anyone living in Spain longer than three months.

**Escritura (Escritura Pública)**  
Public deed executed before a notary. The escritura de compraventa is the title deed for property purchases.

**EX-14 Form**  
Application form for Digital Nomad Visa.

**EX-15 Form**  
Application form for NIE (foreigner identification number).

**EX-17 Form**  
Application form for TIE (residence card).

---

## F

**Finca**  
Rural property or country estate. Also used to refer to any real estate property in legal contexts.

**Foreigners' Office (Oficina de Extranjería)**  
Immigration office handling residence permits, renewals, and related procedures for non-EU citizens.

---

## G

**Gastos de Comunidad**  
Monthly or quarterly community fees paid by apartment owners for maintenance of shared areas, services, and building insurance.

**Gestor/Gestoría**  
Administrative agent who handles bureaucratic procedures on your behalf—tax filings, business registrations, license applications. Very common in Spain.

**Golden Visa**  
Former residency-by-investment program (ended April 2025) that granted Spanish residence to those investing €500,000+ in real estate or other qualifying investments.

---

## I

**IBI (Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles)**  
Annual property tax paid to local municipalities based on cadastral value. Similar to property tax in other countries.

**IPREM (Indicador Público de Renta de Efectos Múltiples)**  
Public income indicator used as reference for calculating minimum income requirements for visas. Adjusted annually for inflation.

**IRNR (Impuesto sobre la Renta de No Residentes)**  
Non-resident income tax. Non-residents owning Spanish property must file annual returns even if the property generates no rental income.

**ITP (Impuesto de Transmisiones Patrimoniales)**  
Transfer tax on resale property purchases, ranging from 6-10% depending on region.

**IVA (Impuesto sobre el Valor Añadido)**  
Value-added tax (VAT). New properties are subject to 10% IVA instead of transfer tax.

---

## L

**Ley de Startups**  
Startup Law (Law 28/2022) that introduced the Digital Nomad Visa and other measures to attract international talent and entrepreneurs.

---

## M

**Modelo 790 Código 012**  
Fee payment form for NIE applications. Costs €9.84 and must be paid at a Spanish bank before your NIE appointment.

---

## N

**NIE (Número de Identificación de Extranjero)**  
Foreigner identification number. Essential for all official transactions in Spain—banking, property purchases, employment, taxes. Required for nearly everything.

**NIF (Número de Identificación Fiscal)**  
Tax identification number. For foreigners, the NIF and NIE are the same number.

**Non-Lucrative Visa**  
Residence permit for financially independent individuals who don't plan to work in Spain. Popular with retirees. Requires €2,400/month passive income.

**Nota Simple**  
Official extract from the Land Registry showing property ownership, boundaries, mortgages, and legal charges. Essential during property due diligence.

**Notario (Notary)**  
Public official who authenticates legal documents and property transactions. Spanish notaries are highly qualified legal professionals appointed by the government.

---

## O

**Obra Nueva**  
New-build property sold directly by a developer that has never been occupied.

**Oficina de Extranjería**  
See Foreigners' Office.

---

## P

**Padrón Municipal**  
Municipal register containing all residents registered in a municipality. Updated through empadronamiento.

**Permiso de Residencia**  
Residence permit or authorization to live in Spain legally.

**Plusvalía Municipal**  
Local capital gains tax charged by municipalities on the increase in land value when property is sold or transferred.

---

## R

**Registro de la Propiedad**  
Land Registry where property ownership, mortgages, and legal charges are officially recorded.

**Residencia**  
Residence or residency status in Spain.

**Resguardo**  
Receipt or proof of submission for administrative applications. Confirms you've filed paperwork while awaiting processing.

---

## S

**Schengen Area**  
26 European countries with open borders allowing free movement. Spanish residence permits grant Schengen travel rights.

**Sede Electrónica**  
Electronic headquarters—official government websites where you can conduct administrative procedures online, book appointments, and submit applications.

**Social Security Number (Número de Afiliación)**  
Social Security registration number required for employment and accessing Spanish healthcare. Different from NIE.

---

## T

**TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero)**  
Foreigner identity card. Physical residence card issued to non-EU citizens with Spanish residency. Must be carried as ID.

**Traductor Jurado**  
Sworn translator officially authorized to provide certified translations for legal documents. Required for apostilled foreign documents.

---

## V

**Visa Nacional**  
National visa (long-stay visa) allowing residence in Spain beyond 90 days. Categories include work visas, student visas, Non-Lucrative Visa, and Digital Nomad Visa.

**Volante de Empadronamiento**  
See Certificado de Empadronamiento.
